Budweiser is an American-style pale lager produced by AB InBev (Brown, 2016). Budweiser was founded in 1876 by Carl Conrad & Co. of St. Louis, Missouri, and has grown to become a major selling beer firm in the United States.

Kronenbourg 1664 is a golden pale lager with a 5.5% ABV in continental Europe and a 5.0% ABV in the United Kingdom ( The Kronenbourg 1664, n.d.). It was first made in 1664 by master brewer Geronimus Hatt at Canon Brewery in the Alsace area of France.

Corona is a beer brand owned by the Belgian firm AB InBev that is made by the Mexican brewery Cervecera Modelo (The USA Today, 2004). It is the most popular imported beer brand in the United States.

Heineken Lager Beer (Dutch: Heineken Pilsener), or simply Heineken, is a 5% ABV pale lager beer produced by the Dutch brewing corporation Heineken N.V (The Heineken Company, n.d.).
